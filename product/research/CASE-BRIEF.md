## Challenge Title

**Agriculture's Next Frontier: Feeding Humans on the Red Planet**

---

## The Mission

Design and prototype an **autonomous AI agent system** capable of managing a **greenhouse on Mars** to supplement the diet of a crew of **4 astronauts** during a **450-day surface-stay mission**.

### The system must:
1. **Monitor and control the environment** — temperature, humidity, light, water
2. **Manage resources** — efficiently use and recycle water and nutrients
3. **Detect and respond to plant stress** — nutrient deficiency, disease, environmental stress
4. **Optimize for growth** — learn and adapt strategies for alien environment

### Optimization goals:
- Maximize nutrient output
- Maintain dietary balance
- Minimize resource consumption

---

## Expected Deliverable

- A **working simulation or digital twin** of the Martian greenhouse
- There **must be a working agent system** behind it
- A **3-minute PowerPoint presentation**

---

## Users

- NASA planners
- Syngenta scientists (less extreme settings)
- Teams designing autonomous cropping systems for Earth

## Data Provided

Knowledge base via MCP endpoint (7 domains):

| # | Domain | Description |
|---|--------|-------------|
| 1 | Mars Environmental Constraints | Gravity, atmosphere, solar, soil, temperature |
| 2 | Controlled Environment Agriculture | Hydroponics, environmental control, lighting |
| 3 | Crop Profiles | Growth cycles, water/nutrient needs, yield, stress response |
| 4 | Plant Stress and Response Guide | Detection and mitigation strategies |
| 5 | Human Nutritional Strategy | Caloric/nutrient requirements for 4 astronauts |
| 6 | Greenhouse Operational Scenarios | Failure modes, crisis events |
| 7 | Innovation Impact (Mars to Earth) | Sustainability and real-world applications |

Participants may also use publicly available datasets.
